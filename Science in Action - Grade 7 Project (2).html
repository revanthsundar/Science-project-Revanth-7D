🧪 Science in Action

Observe, Explore and Reflect

Grade 7 Science Project
Topic 1: Be a Scientist for a Day

⬇

❓ Research Question

Which material melts ice the fastest: Salt, Sugar, or Plain Water?

👀 Observation

People often spread salt on icy roads in cold countries. This made me curious about whether salt melts ice faster than other common materials such as sugar or water.

💡 Hypothesis

I think salt will melt ice faster than sugar or plain water because salt lowers the freezing point of water.

🧰 Materials Required

🧊

Ice Cubes

🥣

3 Bowls

🧂

Salt

🍬

Sugar

💧

Water

⏱️

Timer

🔬 Procedure

  1. Place one ice cube into each bowl.
  2. Add salt to the first bowl.
  3. Add sugar to the second bowl.
  4. Add water to the third bowl.
  5. Start the timer.
  6. Observe the melting process.
  7. Record the time taken for each ice cube to melt.

📊 Observation Table

Material Added Time Taken to Melt
Salt 12 Minutes
Plain Water 15 Minutes
Sugar 18 Minutes

📈 Results Graph

🧂 Salt - 12 min
12
💧 Water - 15 min
15
🍬 Sugar - 18 min
18

✅ Conclusion

Salt melted the ice the fastest. Water came second, while sugar took the longest time. This supports the hypothesis that salt speeds up the melting process.

🤔 Reflection

Through this project, I learned how scientists use observations and experiments to answer questions. I also discovered why salt is commonly used to melt ice on roads and pathways in cold regions.

🌟 Science helps us understand everyday life through observation, investigation, and evidence.
